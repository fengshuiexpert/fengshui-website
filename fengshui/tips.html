What is the objective of Feng Shui?

In simple terms, Feng Shui works to create a harmonious and Yin-Yan balanced flow of Chi in your living space. For example, excessive active energy (Yan energy) in the house might cause irritation and argument in the household. Excessive stationary energy (Yin energy) might cause depression and even suppress the occupants' luck.

An expert practitioner can “see” the ebb and flow of Chi in your surroundings and find ways to strengthen and channel it for your benefit.

What is the Ba-Gua?

The Ba-Gua is the "compass" or map that is superimposed on your property and your house to help you locate areas on your property or in your house for career, helpful people, children, creativity, marriage, recognition, financial success, family, knowledge and self-enlightenment. Feng Shui problems reflect issues in the occupants' lives. For example, in a home where the marriage area is missing or has blocking energy, the occupant may have a difficult time in a relationship or may not have a relationship.

An expert practitioner creates a Ba-Gua that you can use as a guide to to develop awareness of the significance of your surroundings. It is the foundation for applying Feng Shui remedies that are appropriate to your objectives.

Some examples of Feng Shui remedies

Remedies such as crystals, mirrors or bamboo flutes are called transcendental cures (non-logical, non-scientific cures). Though they may seem "magical," they serve as a display to remind you to cultivate good intentions for your goals in the area on which you are working.
